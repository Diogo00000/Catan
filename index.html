How to Play

Settlers of Catan - Quick Guide

Goal

Be the first player to reach 10 victory points. Points come from your settlements and cities on the board, plus the special awards and Victory Point cards described below. The game ends the instant someone reaches 10 on their turn.

Setup

Play happens in snake order. Going around the table once, each player places a settlement and a road. Then, in reverse order, each player places a second settlement and road. So everyone ends up with 2 settlements and 2 roads before normal turns begin.

  • Your second settlement immediately earns you one resource from each hex touching it, which will be your starting hand.
  • The usual placement rules apply during setup. However, your first two roads and settlements don't yet need to connect to anything.

The Board

The island is a ring of hexes. Each producing hex shows a resource and a number token. When that number is rolled, the hex produces its resource for every settlement and city touching it.

  • Resources: wood, brick, sheep, wheat and ore.
  • The desert produces nothing and is where the robber starts.
  • With 5 or 6 players, the larger extension board is used, with more hexes and number tokens.

A Turn

  • (Optional) Play one development card. You may do this before rolling.
  • Roll the dice. Every settlement on a matching hex collects 1 resource, and every city collects 2.
  • Build, trade, and/or play a development card (if you haven't already played one this turn).
  • End your turn and pass to the next player.

Only one development card may be played per turn, before or after the roll, and never on the same turn you bought it.

Rolling a 7

  • No resources are produced.
  • Every player holding more than 7 cards discards half (rounded down) back to the bank.
  • The roller moves the robber to a new hex and steals one random card from an opponent with a settlement or city touching that hex.
  • The robber blocks its hex from producing anything until it is moved again (by another 7 or a Knight).

Development card
  • A settlement must connect to one of your own roads, and can't sit next to another settlement or city. There must be at least two empty intersections or roads between them.
  • A city upgrades one of your existing settlements, and then collects 2 of each matching resource instead of 1.

Victory Points

  • Settlement: 1 point each.
  • City: 2 points each.
  • Longest Road (starting at a sequence of five continuous roads): 2 points.
  • Largest Army (starting at three played Knight cards): 2 points.
  • Victory Point card: 1 point each (kept hidden until you win).

Trading

You may trade on your turn, after rolling:

  • Bank - trade 4 identical resources for any 1 resource (4:1).
  • Harbours - 3:1 on a generic harbour, or 2:1 for a specific resource, if you have a settlement or city on that harbour.
  • Players - make direct trades with other players for any resources you both agree on.

Development Cards

  • Knight: move the robber and steal a card, just like rolling a 7. Playing 3 Knights earns Largest Army.
  • Victory Point: plus 1 victory point, kept hidden until you win.
  • Road Building: place 2 roads for free.
  • Year of Plenty: take any 2 resources from the bank.
  • Monopoly: name one resource, every other player hands you all of theirs.

Longest Road

The first player to build a continuous road of 5 or more segments takes the Longest Road award, which earns an extra 2 VP. It passes to any player who later builds a strictly longer road.

Largest Army

The first player to play 3 Knights takes the Largest Army award, which earns an extra 2 VP. It passes to any player who later plays more Knights.

The Bank

The bank holds 19 of each resource. If a roll would pay out more of a resource than remains, then a single owed player takes whatever is left. However, if several players are owed that resource and there isn't enough for all of them, none of them receive it.
